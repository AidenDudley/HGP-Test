LeBron's oustanding career

14-time all-star

Unprivelaged child

For starters, most would say LeBron James had among the worst childhoods possible. LeBron was born in Akron, Ohio on December 30, 1984 to 16 year old Gloria James. Gloria was in high school when LeBron was born. His father was a 44 year-old ex-prisoner and ran off on the two because he was uninterested in having a family. To this day, LeBron goes by his mothers last name "James."

Highschool Hopes

LeBron was an excellent athlete from the start. He was recruited to the high school of St. Vincent-St. Mary's. There he developed a name for himself as an extaordinary basketball and football player. In addition to basketball, LeBron played on the St. Vincent-St. Mary's varsity football team. As a matter of fact, LeBron was so good at football, D1 colleges offered him full ride football scholarships. By the end of LeBron's highschool, he had over 10 awards for basketball, and over 5 awards for football. Basically, LeBron was in the drivers seat and didn't have to bend to anyone as to what he wants to do with his life. In the 2003 NBA Draft, the Cleveland Cavaliers had the first overall pick and offered LeBron a $19 million contract to skip college and go straight to the NBA. LeBron couldn't deny the offer so he decided to skip college and go straight to the NBA.

Rookie Sensation

Before LeBron played an official NBA game, he signed to a $90 million deal with Nike and had multiple other endorsement contracts with many other companies. He was running the show when he hadn't even played a game yet.

LeBron's first NBA game was against the Sacramento Kings. In that game he scored 25 points, 9 assists, 6 boards, and 4 steals. In his rookie season he would go on to become the third player to average at least 20 points, 5 assists, and 5 rebounds in their rookie season. Along with this great accomplishment, he lead the Cavaliers to their first NBA championship in franchise history. They made it to the second round, but then lost.

The Rest of his Accomplishments

LeBron has had 15 stellar years in the NBA and isn't done yet. Last season he averaged about 26 points, 9 assists, and 9 rebounds per game. Not only does LeBron lead his team in points and assists, but he also led his team to its third NBA Finals in a row and his 8 NBA Finals in his career. Also, this past play-off run was his 12th and his 8th championship run. Also did you know evert time LeBron has entered the playoffs, he has never been eliminated in the first round.

Other records LeBron James has set are:

  • Only player in NBA History to record at least 28,000 points, 7,000 assists, and 7,00 rebounds in career
  • Only player in NBA History to be awarded with the Player of the Month Award 4 times in 2 consecutive seasons
  • Only player in NBA History to average at least 28 points, 6 assist, and 6 rebounds per game for 13 seasons
  • Only player in NBA History to be in top 10 in career points and top 15 in career assists.
  • Only player in NBA History to average at least 25 points, 8 assist, and 8 rebounds per game and shoot .540 from the field in a single season(2016-2017 season)
  • One of two players in NBA History to win at least 2 different NBA MVP Awards with two different franchises
  • One of four players in NBA history to win NBA MVP with a team, leave, and then come back.
  • Only player in NBA history to post 30 or more points and shoot over 60 percent for six consecutive games in a single season.
  • One of five players in NBA history to score 50+ points multiple times for two different teams.
  • 1st place all-time in career assists by a forward with (7461).
